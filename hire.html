Prototyping
Starting off with a prototype alone puts a team ahead of most. Prototypes keep expenses low and allow for lean, flexible and methodical development. One of the common problems I see is that the foundation of the game is not flexible enough to allow for changes required by the designer, undoing the benefit of prototyping altogether.

This becomes an interation hell if there is ample motivation from analytics data to make a design change, but changes are made with ad hoc hacks and eventually result in a rigid codebase. Teams either go for an expensive redevelopment, or more commonly just give up on updating their game.

From a coding point of view, I usually approach this with the development of highly generic and composable modules, that are can be combined in ways to produce wide ranging behaviours. These typically also goes into a wide, use-case neutral codebase as well, an investment that leads to massive returns over time.
