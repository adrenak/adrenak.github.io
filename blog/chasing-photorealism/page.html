Much of my game development work since I started has been on mobile. While I've certainly had fun doing that, I have realized that it has kept me from pushing myself further as far as pursuing graphical realism goes.

When it was released, people told me that Speed Street: Tokyo had pretty good graphics for a mobile game in 2014.

Speed Street: Tokyo was okay.

But there's the problem right there.

It is only pretty good. For a mobile game. In 2014.

I recently thought of diversifying into architectural visualisations, especially because there is a lot of demand for them owing to the VR craze. Now, these visualizations need to be realistic looking, so I looked up some tutorials and found a nice timelapse video here. Below is the end result of the video.

Such skills. Much wow.
I was impressed. The video creater managed to assemble that in less than half an hour, assuming the video is sped up 4x. But then, I thought I also could do this. After all it is just a sequence of steps in a software I am already so familiar with. By 4 am (yeah, I started after dinner) I had managed to make a pretty good replica of the tutorial and also an original layout of my own.

I spent the week finding time whenever I could after work to make a few more. I got some models from archive3d.net. Problem is, all the models there are high poly and I end up with, like half a million polygons on the screen. Add lighting and the polycount goes up quickly.

So, I take the pre-made models and try them in the scene one by one. Then I just make low poly replicas. After all I am not a furniture designer. In a week, I managed to make 3 environments. I like the hotel room the most.

A random apartment. Forgot to plan a kitchen and bathroom. So I call it an "apartment section".
>
An art galery. Made this for a client.
A hotel I stayed at last weekend in Bangalore. They had a full fledged mini kitchen. What economy budget hotel gives you that? So obviously I had to make this.
After the week ended today, I made my Behance account and added these there. Check it out for more images of these environments. If I get good enough at making more realistic environments, I might write a guide sometime.

I like the idea of chasing photorealism, mostly because I want to make more of these real estate visualization things. However, when it comes to games, I remain indifferent. As far as my preference is concerned, I would rather see a good art style.

For instance, I played Indigo Child yesterday.

Does it look realistic? No.

Did I love it? Yes. Very much.
